More About Me

One of my favorite hobbies is watching and taking part in sports.

During my years in secondary school I began to develop an interest in American sports, particularly basketball and American football. This interest came about after I watched the movie Moneyball which is the story about the introduction of advanced analytics in baseball. I had always been interested in the analytical and numbers based approach to improving sporting performance and seeing the application of analytics in these sports attracted me to them. I thoroughly enjoy watching the many dedicated YouTube accounts who dive deep into the numbers to provide unbaised insights into players performance.

Another passion of mine is grappling. I've always been interested in combat sports and have recently taking up training. My favorite part of the sport is the data driven community surrounding it. Being such a broad discipline there is devoted community researching the most effective and ineffective techniques to study, in order to optimise there training time. There have even been databases built to research particular techniques and the rate at which they work.

As mentioned above there are many accounts dedicated to exploring the analytical side of these sports. The JxmyHighroller YouTube account is a prime example of using data analytics to tell a story. Each of video is dedicated to telling a story about a player or event using data visualisations to enchance story telling. The Thinking Basketball YouTube account takes a more analytical approach using statistical analysis combined with game footage to analyse player and team performance. Less Impressed More Involved BJJ has created an online database similar to an SQL database. Compiling grappling matches to enable searching using technique or athlete names. Although the database is behind a paywall the YouTube account provides valuable data insights.
